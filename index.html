Why this patient, why this ward

Respiratory disease in DC tracks the same ward geography as cardiovascular disease. Asthma hospitalization rates for Black children in Ward 8 are roughly four times the rate in Ward 3. Iron-deficiency anemia — one of the things you'll see in Ms. Carter's CBC today — is also two to three times more common in our east-of-the-river wards than west.

These aren't accidents. They're the downstream effects of housing quality, environmental exposure, food access, and the specific history of medical neglect in specific neighborhoods. The vocabulary you learn this week is the vocabulary Ms. Carter's doctors use every day to help her. Learn it like she's someone you know.

Lungs → Oxygen → Blood

Three frameworks, one system. The heart pumps. The lungs oxygenate. The blood carries. If any one fails, the other two can't compensate.

1. Breath Sounds — what you hear and what it means

Six sounds. Six different stories about what's happening in the airways. Master the discontinuous/continuous distinction first — it's the easiest way in.

SoundCharacterOften Indicates
NormalClear, smooth air movement bilaterally.Healthy lungs — no intervention needed.
Crackles (rales)Discontinuous popping. Like velcro separating.Fluid in alveoli (pulmonary edema, pneumonia).
WheezeContinuous high-pitched whistle. Worse on expiration.Narrowed airways (asthma, bronchospasm).
RhonchiContinuous low-pitched rumbling. Often clears with cough.Mucus in larger airways (bronchitis).
StridorContinuous high-pitched, mostly on inspiration.Upper airway obstruction — EMERGENCY.
DiminishedQuiet, hard to hear.Atelectasis, pleural effusion, pneumothorax.

2. Pulse Oximetry — reading the number on the finger

SpO₂ is non-invasive, bedside, instant. Every nurse on every shift documents it. Know the thresholds cold.

The Two Confused Pairs — don't mix these up

These are the most common W8 mistakes every year. Get them right now, before the Boards.

Thrombosis

thromb/o (clot) + -osis (condition)
A clot that FORMS IN PLACE and stays put. Deep vein thrombosis (DVT) = clot in the deep veins of the leg.
Test: If the clot hasn't moved, it's thrombosis.

Embolism

embol/o (plug) + -ism
A clot that MOVES and lodges somewhere else. Pulmonary embolism = a clot traveled from somewhere (usually a DVT) to the lungs.
Test: Could it have traveled? If yes = embolism.

Crackles (rales)

Onomatopoeia — the sound
DISCONTINUOUS popping sounds. Like velcro pulling apart. Individual pops, not continuous.
Test: Can you count individual pops? It's crackles.

Wheeze

Middle English — to breathe hard
CONTINUOUS high-pitched whistle. Sustained note, usually worse on expiration.
Test: Is it a sustained whistle? It's wheeze.

Wednesday · OR/Clinic Lab

Phlebotomy Simulation Lab

Wednesday's 80-minute lab. Three stations. You'll draw Ms. Carter's blood (simulated) and document it in EHR Entry 6.

This is the most hands-on lab of Phase 3.

Real equipment: phlebotomy training arms, infant heel stick simulators, 21-gauge vacutainer needles, 23-gauge butterflies, color-coded tubes, real alcohol wipes and gauze. You'll rotate through three stations. No real venipuncture on humans — training arms only — but everything else is the real clinical workflow.

⚠ Department Orientation — before any equipment comes out

  • Needles and lancets are CLINICAL SIMULATION TOOLS. Not toys. Not pranks. Never at another student or at yourself.
  • NEVER perform venipuncture on a real person in this lab. Training arms and infant simulators only.
  • Gloves on BEFORE touching any equipment. Off BEFORE touching your face, phone, or common surfaces.
  • ALL sharps go in the sharps container IMMEDIATELY after use. No "I'll put it there in a minute."
  • Access to the phlebotomy arms is tied to professional conduct. Real phlebotomists complete a department orientation before their first draw — you're doing the same.
Station A

Adult Venipuncture

Full 14-step vacutainer draw on the training arm. Use Ms. Carter's patient info for tube labels. Three tubes in correct order: light blue (D-dimer), gold SST (BMP), lavender EDTA (CBC).

⏱ 25 min · rotate through 3 roles: phlebotomist, documenter, evaluator
Station B

Infant Heel Stick

9-step heel stick on the infant simulator. Safe zones only (medial/lateral plantar). Warming, cleansing, lancing, collecting onto microtainer or Guthrie card. Do NOT milk the heel.

⏱ 20 min · kinesthetic precision + developmental awareness
Station C

Tube ID + Chain of Custody

Label 5 required fields on each tube. Match tubes to the lab requisition. Catch the ONE intentionally mislabeled tube built into the kit. Labeling is a safety step, not a clerical one.

⏱ 15 min · accuracy + error-catching
